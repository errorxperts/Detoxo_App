# Detoxo — Product Overview

**Stop the endless scroll. Take your time back.**

Detoxo is an Android app that spots Reels, Shorts, and other infinite-feed videos
*inside the apps you already use* — Instagram, YouTube, Snapchat, WhatsApp, and
more — and gently pulls you back out before you lose another hour. It also counts
every short video you watch, so you finally see the number you never wanted to
admit.

---

## The problem

Short-form video is designed to be bottomless. There's no episode to finish, no
end of the page — just one more clip, then one more, then twenty minutes gone.
You didn't *decide* to watch 80 reels tonight. The feed decided for you.

Most "screen-time" tools only tell you about it the next morning, in a chart you
close and forget. By then the time is already spent.

## The solution

Detoxo works **in the moment**. The second a reel or short starts playing, Detoxo
notices and acts — pressing back, closing the feed, or (if you choose) locking the
screen. You stay in the app you opened for a reason; you just don't fall down the
rabbit hole. And because it runs quietly in the background, it keeps protecting you
even when Detoxo itself is closed.

No willpower required. No shame charts. Just a firm, friendly nudge exactly when
you need it.

---

## Why Detoxo is different

| Generic screen-time apps | Detoxo |
|---|---|
| Show you a report **after** the time is gone | Steps in **while** you're scrolling |
| Block a whole app (all-or-nothing) | Blocks the **reels**, not the app — you can still message, search, and post |
| One blunt "app timer" | Flexible **plans**: block everything, earn watch-time by staying off, allow one clip, or take a short break |
| Easy to ignore or turn off | Optional **PIN lock** and uninstall protection keep future-you honest |
| Just tracking | Tracking **and** intervention **and** an at-a-glance counter on your home screen |

The key idea: **you don't have to quit the apps you love — you just quit the
bottomless part.** You can still DM a friend on Instagram or look up a how-to on
YouTube. Detoxo only steps in when the endless video feed takes over.

## Feature highlights

### Reel & Short blocking
Detoxo detects short-form video surfaces across popular apps and pulls you out the
instant a feed starts. It focuses on the infinite-scroll parts — Reels, Shorts, and
similar — while leaving the rest of the app usable.

### Blocking plans — pick your style
Detoxo gives you four ways to handle the scroll, so it fits how *you* want to
change:

- **Block All** — the strict setting. Every reel gets closed the moment it appears.
  Best for a clean break.
- **Conscious** — *earn-as-you-abstain.* Instead of a hard wall, you build up a
  small bank of watch-time by staying **off** reels. Roughly every 10 minutes you
  spend away from the feed earns you about 1 minute of guilt-free watching (up to a
  10-minute cap). Dip into reels and the bank counts down; when it hits zero, Detoxo
  eases you back out until you've earned more. It turns "just say no" into a gentle
  give-and-take that rewards patience.
- **One Reel** — feeling the itch? This lets you watch a single clip, then closes the
  feed so one doesn't become fifty. Tap it again for one more.
- **Unblock** — want a handful, not just one? Pick how many reels to release (a dial
  from 2 to 20), watch them, and your base mode returns until you unlock more.
- **Pause** — need the feed for a few minutes (finding a specific video, showing a
  friend)? Tap **Pause**, pick a short window (2–10 minutes), and blocking steps
  aside. When the timer runs out, protection switches itself back on automatically —
  no way to "forget" to re-enable it.

> A note on the name: in the app this earn-as-you-go mode is called **Conscious** —
> a mindful middle ground between all-or-nothing.

### On-device Reel Counter — with a floating bubble and home-screen widget
Awareness is half the battle, so Detoxo **counts the reels you watch** — today and
all-time — completely on your device. The counter runs on its own and keeps tallying
even when blocking is turned off or paused, so you always get an honest number.

- A **floating bubble** can hover over your feed while you scroll, showing today's
  count ticking up in real time. Drag it anywhere; it snaps neatly to the edge of the
  screen and stays out of your way. Tap it to jump into Detoxo. During a **One Reel**
  or **Unblock** session it doubles as an unlock countdown — showing how many reels
  you have left before it flips back to the daily total.
- A **home-screen widget** puts "reels today" and your all-time total right on your
  launcher, so the number greets you every time you unlock your phone.
- A quick-scroll heuristic keeps the count honest: a video only counts once you've
  actually watched it for about 2 seconds — flicking past clips doesn't inflate the
  number.

You can restyle both the bubble and the widget — different looks, sizes, colors, and
an optional "the more you watch, the redder it gets" usage tint — so your counter
matches your home screen and quietly reflects your habits.

### App & website blocking, and daily limits
Beyond reels, Detoxo lets you build a **blocklist** of distracting apps and websites.
The website blocker watches your browser's address bar and closes blocked sites
(including an optional built-in adult-content list). You can also set **daily limits**
to cap how long you spend before Detoxo steps in.

### PIN lock & staying-honest protection
Set a **PIN** to guard Detoxo's settings so a moment of weakness can't undo your
progress. Unlock with your fingerprint or face where supported, with a lockout after
too many wrong tries. Optional device-admin protection makes Detoxo harder to uninstall
on impulse — a real commitment device for future-you.

### Runs quietly, always on
Once set up, Detoxo does its job in the background with a small status notification so
you always know it's watching your back. It keeps working even if you swipe the app
away, and comes back after a restart.

## Getting started (Android)

1. Open Detoxo and follow the short walkthrough.
2. Grant two key permissions — **Accessibility** (so Detoxo can see when a reel is on
   screen) and **Display over apps** (so the bubble and nudges can appear).
3. Choose which apps to protect on the **Blocklist**, pick a plan, and you're done.

> **Android only.** Detoxo relies on Android's Accessibility features, which iPhones
> don't offer, so it isn't available on iOS.
